## What Went Well

- **Zero production incidents.** The two Epic 1 process fixes — hazard-named-AC-gets-a-hazard-named-test and `[review] trigger = "always"` — paid for themselves; every story carries red-then-green proof on its named hazard.
- **Epic 1 groundwork cashed in** (Luca's words): the deferred-work ledger, the review-always gate, and the pure-core seams all did real work this epic.
- **The pure-core write-function pattern** (`applyPlayStatusChange`, 2.1) became the template for all four write paths — and it's why the mid-epic FR-2 inversion (platinum-only auto-hide) was a one-function change.
- **`useTrackingMutations`** extracted in 2.3 became the single mutation seam; 2.4/2.5 extended it rather than hand-rolling. The "two surfaces must never disagree" bet held.
- **Deferred-work handoff worked as designed:** 2.3's review predicted the focus-trap selector would miss the form controls 2.4 was about to add; 2.4 closed exactly that hole with the shared `FOCUSABLE_SELECTOR`.
- **Review layers earned their keep:** the 2.3 HIGH (panel actions unmounting the card under an open dialog) and 2.4 HIGH (date inputs PATCHing per keystroke) would both have been ugly in production.
- **Mid-epic product revisions were the process working, not failing** — `platinum-only-auto-hide` amended FR-2 because real daily use contradicted the spec; no automation catches "stop hiding my game, I'm still chasing the platinum."

## Challenges

1. **jsdom-only UI verification** — flagged in 2.1, 2.2, and 2.3 (popover anchoring, portal layering, breakpoints, hit areas untested by a real layout engine). The card-info-stack revision spec plus the manual test run's fixes (popover viewport-flip, uniform card heights, toast durations) are the bill coming due.
2. **Integration tests email Luca a real magic link on every local run.** Root cause: `vitest-pool-workers` loads `.dev.vars` as bindings, so local tests get a real `RESEND_API_KEY` and `providers/email.ts` selects the real Resend provider. The `vitest.config.ts` comment claiming secrets "don't exist in the test environment" is only true in CI.
3. **UTC date stamping** — deferred in 2.1, stakes raised in 2.2 (date user-visible and immutable) and 2.4, never resolved in-epic. Evening changes west of Greenwich permanently record tomorrow's date on write-once fields. Biggest unpaid debt in the ledger. **Decision made this retro** (see action items).
4. **Dropped/milestone-only games are unreachable in the UI** until Epic 3's reveal pills (search-select is a no-op; its wiring is already Story 6.1's first AC, FR-42).
5. **bmad-loop on Windows** — resolved by the orchestrator pivot above.

## Significant Discoveries

No discoveries invalidate Epic 3's plan. Two product decisions made in this retro amend existing requirements:

- **FR-18 amendment:** the owned-before-wishlisted tier applies to **all** shelf views, filtered/reveal views included. Document in epics.md/PRD before Story 3.1 (closes the `spec-shelf-order-owned-tier` open question).
- **Timezone policy (binds FR-44/45, FR-6 stamp sites):** capture the browser timezone into `SETTING` at first login; user-editable in Settings; all four date-stamp sites compute "today" in that zone.

One new product idea captured for triage, **not** decided here: **permanently delete a game** (possibly only from `Dropped`) so the library can be cleaned of never-again games. Cuts against the append-only library assumption that Epic 4's sync relies on (a deleted game would resync); needs a real decision (hard delete vs. archived/never flag, sync semantics). Candidate neighborhood: Epic 6 chores. Owner: Luca + John.

> **Post-retro decision (2026-07-10, Luca + John): no feature — `Dropped` as it stands is the answer.** Hard delete rejected: any PSN-library game resyncs (FR-33 creates what it can't match), and a tombstone table is an archive flag in disguise. Irreversible soft delete rejected: re-adding the game (Story 6.1) matches the soft-deleted row via FR-34 / the unique `(source, external_id)` constraint, forcing revival — so "irreversible" is a contradiction; any honest version collapses into archive. The pain the feature targets (a noisy Dropped reveal) sits on a surface that hasn't shipped (Epic 3) and has never been felt. Revisit trigger: if the Dropped reveal accumulates real never-again noise in daily use, the ready design is a `game_tracking`-level `archived_on` date — hidden everywhere server-side, revive-on-re-add with milestone dates standing, sync semantics untouched — as its own Epic 6 story, never ACs bolted onto 6.3 (see DW-6).
