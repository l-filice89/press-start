## What went well

- **Probe-first economics (Luca's headline).** The probes let us use what we already had to great extent, saving another external surface and more work: two coverage probes deleted two fallback providers from the plan (scores 96.9%, TTB 95.4% — OpenCritic and HLTB never written), and 10.4's probe HALTed the story *before* code when the category id proved undiscoverable — which is exactly what forced the better design.
- **The endTime pivot upgraded the feature.** Luca supplied one anchor game (Risk of Rain 2, departing Jul 21); probing its store pricing found the PS_PLUS inclusion offer's `endTime` — an exact per-game departure date on an anonymous endpoint. That turned 10.2's post-hoc "LEFT PS+" stamp into a dated pre-departure "LEAVING {date}" warning and retired the old pill outright. A blocked story shipped the best version of itself.
- **The rule pipeline held under a new surface.** PROBE-BEFORE-YOU-MAP fired first-task in 10.1/10.3/10.4; SAMPLE-OF-ONE was satisfied (TTB 62:62, endTime distribution probed over 11 games); BUDGET-COUNTS-EVERY-SUBREQUEST ledgers were written at every dispatch site; the follow-up-review contract's one firing paid for itself with a new medium.
- **Review rigor both ways.** 31 findings rejected with evidence — reviewers verified against reality instead of auto-patching (the Epic 11 discipline), while all 3 HIGHs (discount-branding false positive, rotation livelock, budget bust) were caught pre-merge in 10.4's pass.

## Challenges / lessons

1. **UI was the weak point — placement guessed, then chased (Luca's central finding).** Every feature was behaviorally correct at review, and then: four layout-churn commits (detail panel re-stack, card row compaction, leaving banner into the header, ScoreBadges moved under the cover), an entire follow-on spec because 10.4's first cut put the leaving date only on the card, and 10.5's one medium was the add modal's PRIMARY path showing no scores. Specs pin behavior precisely and presentation loosely; the gap got closed by the most expensive loop available (build → Luca reacts → rework). → **UI-MOCK-GATE rule, shipped this session** (action item 1).
2. **Preserve-vs-clear was the epic's hazard class** — 4 of 12 mediums were "absent field conflated with explicit null": rematch wiping scores (10.1), rematch inheriting stale TTB forever (10.3, follow-up-review find), hollow-200 clearing departure dates (10.4). Reviews caught every instance, but the class recurred because nothing forces a spec to state per-field absent-vs-null semantics up front. → action item 3 (candidate rule).
3. **`200 []` needs a per-endpoint ruling, not just a guard.** DEGENERATE-RESPONSE says guard the empty reply; it doesn't say *which way*. 10.1 scores: empty = fail closed. 10.3 TTB: empty = normal absence — we guessed fail-closed first and shipped a permanently-lit, unclearable FR-40 banner into review. Folded into action item 3's rule shape.
4. **Budget arithmetic drifted every story** — honest at spec time, re-counted under review in 10.1/10.2/10.4; the 10.4 HIGH (score refresh stacking on a sweep invocation, >50) is the rule working only because review re-ran the numbers. The rule holds; the drift is the cost of an evolving cron rotation.

## Key takeaways

1. **A probe is the cheapest feature you'll ever ship.** Two providers deleted from the plan by coverage numbers, one blocked story upgraded into a better design — all before code. Probe-first is now the epic's defining economic pattern, not just a safety rule.
2. **Behavioral correctness is not doneness — placement is spec too.** An epic can pass every review and still spend a day chasing where things sit. The mock is minutes; the chase is commits. UI-MOCK-GATE makes placement a gated artifact like risk and architecture already are.
3. **Write-path semantics need to be stated, not inferred.** Absent-vs-null and empty-vs-failure were re-derived per story and got guessed wrong under review pressure. A rule that forces the spec to declare them turns four mediums into zero.
4. **The follow-up-review contract earns its keep on shared files.** One joint pass over 10.1+10.3's overlapping write path found the defect both per-story reviews structurally couldn't (it lived in the seam).
5. **Emergency epics don't break the pipeline — they feed it.** Epic 11's rule (born from the account lock) gated this epic's every external decision one day later, and the pivot it forced shipped a better feature than the original plan.
