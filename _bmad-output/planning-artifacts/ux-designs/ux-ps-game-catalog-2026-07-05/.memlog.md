- (decision) Catalog card has THREE states, not two (2026-07-14, Luca asked how an already-owned game reads in the catalog). The whole catalog renders including tracked games - hiding them would read as a missing game. States are keyed on the REMAINING ACTION: (1) not tracked -> '+ Add' + 'Claim now'; (2) tracked but NOT owned -> 'In library' marker (cyan) AND 'Claim now' STILL LIVE, because a game added from the catalog is written owned:false/ownership_type:'ps_plus' - it is on the shelf but not yet claimed to the PlayStation account, so dropping Claim at 'tracked' would strand exactly the games the catalog just added; (3) tracked AND owned (purchased or already claimed) -> 'Owned' marker (silver, the established earned/yours meaning) and NO actions. Explicitly NOT the shelf's status pill: that shows PLAY STATE, a tracked-game concept, and reusing it would leak shelf affordances into a destination whose rows are not tracked games (AD-24). Claim success is never guessed - the app cannot observe the PS Store tab; story 6.4's claim/purchase ownership path takes over on the next sync (AD-10).
- (override) CORRECTION 2026-07-14: the catalog card's three states stand, but state (2) was justified with an invented value. A catalog-added game is simply NOT OWNED — i.e. wishlisted (wishlisted = !owned, shipped) AND playable-now (its PS+ flag is on). There is no 'ps_plus' ownership type. 'Claim now' stays live at state (2) because the game is on the shelf but unclaimed on PSN; ownership flips only when a SYNC observes the entitlement (owned_via='membership', story 6.4) — the app cannot see the PS Store tab and never infers a successful claim. - (decision) Catalog ordering (2026-07-14, Luca): NEWEST FIRST (the store payload is already sorted productReleaseDate descending, so it is free), with NO OWNERSHIP TIER. Rationale, in his words: the catalog is a discovery tool, not a "what do I have that I can play today" tool - that is the shelf task. The shelf sorts owned-before-wishlisted (core/shelf.ts) precisely because it answers a different question; hoisting owned games in the catalog would make a discovery surface open on the things already discovered. Tracked games are neither hoisted nor hidden nor dimmed - they sit where they fall with their In library / Owned marker. EXPLICIT ANTI-INSTRUCTION for 7.2: do NOT reuse the shelf comparator. The story says "reuse the shelf card chrome", which is exactly the nudge that would drag the comparator along with it - the risk is real, not hypothetical.
- (override) Catalog ordering REVISED (2026-07-14, Luca): ALPHABETICAL by title, not newest-first. He pointed out the shelf is already alphabetical once you disregard the state and owned tiers - compareShelf in core/shelf.ts is state -> owned -> title.localeCompare(sensitivity base) - and he wants the same thing in the catalog. So the catalog sort is exactly that final tiebreaker with the two tiers removed: case-insensitive, locale-aware A-Z, no state tier, no ownership tier. Supersedes my newest-first proposal, which reached for the store payload productReleaseDate sort because it was free rather than because it was consistent; A-Z is how you scan ~490 games and find your way back to one. Tracked games are not hoisted, hidden, or dimmed - they sit in alphabetical order with their In library / Owned marker. For 7.2: reuse compareShelf s TITLE COMPARISON, never compareShelf itself.
